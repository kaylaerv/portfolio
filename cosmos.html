Cosmos Day Tours Website & Branding

Cosmos Day Tours, a touring company needed a website that would allow it's audience to learn about the newfound company and book tours online. They wanted to demonstrate that they provide tours to only the best sustainable wineries that have friendly and knowledgeable employees. They wanted to appeal not only to people new to the wine touring experience but also people who are wine connoisseurs. They give tours to families and people of all ages; They even do tours that aren't about wine.

My Role

I was responsible for the entire website and identity design. I worked with 2 colleagues who were responsible for photography and website development.

Tools used

  • Sketch
  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Adobe InDesign

Process

It was essential from the beginning to understand Cosmos Day Tours' mission and target audience. We wanted to appeal primarily to tourists between the ages of 30-40 who have money to indulge on weekend tours. The mission of Cosmos Day Tours was "To provide tourist and locals, safe and reliable transportation to wineries, farms, and breweries. To lead the shuttle and tour industry in sustainable, clean air methods of operation."

To start off, the client took my colleagues and I on a wine touring experience early on so that we could better understand what the company and it's services. We experienced 3 different wineries and got a good sense of the atmosphere of the wineries and the people who visited them.

I researched 5 wine touring companies to see what opportunities we could set Cosmos Day Tours apart from the rest.

Positive trends

  • Clear call-to-actions
  • High quality photography and videos
  • Short & concise copy
  • Reviews & testimonials
  • A quick and seamless booking process

Negative Trends

  • Cliche graphics
  • Illegible or hard to read text
  • Cluttered pages lacking negative space
  • Unorganized or confusing booking process

From this research I gained insight into ways we could separate Cosmos Day Tours apart from the competition.

Mood boards were created to get a feel for the photography, colors and typography the client envisioned for the brand.

From the research of competition and the clients requests, I created a site architecture, then the information architecture for each page of the website.

I created wireframes for each template page of the website. With the creation of a visual style guide and wireframes, high fidelity mockups weren't necessary for more than a few of the pages.

The final design conveys 3 major attributes of the Cosmos Day Tours brand: adventure, relaxation, and environmental sustainability.

Conclusion

The client was very happy with the website design and asked for my help with the new Cosmos Day Tours logo and other branding materials. I would have liked to have had time to be able to do more research of the target audience; Learning directly from them about their needs, wants, and pain points would have been beneficial to the process.
